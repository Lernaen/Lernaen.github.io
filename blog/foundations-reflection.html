What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

I project far more confidence than I realise or feel, I have a tendency to get distracted easily, and my ego is often suppressed to help the feelings of others.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

being able to know when you need to set boundaries, when you need to engage with your colleagues, and knowing your limits. Trying to keep it short and sweet, but their roles are basically integral to learning.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

quite how much of the curriculum I've spent the last 5 years teaching myself, thoug usually it's done in a nonverbal fashion on my end

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Probably maintaining self-reflection exercises as a daily thing, and putting down the things I find positive in myself down in text form for others' to read.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because it's frankly the most important thing to learn about functioning in a workplace, far more than technical ability (I won't apply this universally, but I can learn pretty much any journeyman level technical skill, but functioning among other humans is really difficult for me)

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, the Human skills are essentially a vocational training session for functioning in a workplace setting, and I'm picking up the programming skills, but also getting used to working in a collaborative setting is necessary, particularly getting used to submitting imperfect work, and talking my work over with colleagues.
